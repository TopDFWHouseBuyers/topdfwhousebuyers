The True Cost of Traditional Home Sales in Plano

When selling through a real estate agent in Plano, many homeowners don't realize the total costs involved until they're deep in the process. Understanding these expenses helps you compare options fairly.

Real Estate Commissions

In the DFW market, real estate commissions typically total 5-6% of the sale price. On a $400,000 Plano home, that's $20,000-$24,000 in commissions alone.

Repairs and Updates

Agents often recommend repairs and updates to maximize sale price. Fresh paint, new carpeting, landscaping, and minor repairs can easily cost $10,000-$30,000 or more, depending on your home's condition.

Carrying Costs

Every month your house sits on the market, you're paying the mortgage, property taxes, insurance, and utilities. In Plano, with property taxes averaging 2.5% annually, these carrying costs add up quickly.

Closing Costs

Sellers typically pay various closing costs including title policies, transfer taxes, and often contribute to the buyer's closing costs—another 2-3% of the sale price.

Unknown Repair Requests

After the home inspection, buyers often request repairs or price reductions. These unexpected costs can derail your budget and timeline.

When you add everything up, traditional sales often cost 10-15% of your home's value, plus months of time and stress. Cash sales eliminate virtually all of these costs and uncertainties.

Special Considerations for Plano Property Owners

Texas doesn't have state income tax, but property taxes are significant. When selling, understand that property taxes are prorated at closing. If you're behind on taxes, some cash buyers can address this in the purchase agreement.

HOA Properties

Many Plano neighborhoods have homeowners associations. If you're behind on HOA dues or facing fines for property condition, cash buyers typically handle these issues as part of the purchase.
