What Does "Selling As-Is" Actually Mean?

When you sell a home as-is, you're selling it in its current condition without making any repairs or improvements. The buyer accepts the property exactly as it stands, which means you won't need to:

  • Fix foundation issues or structural problems
  • Repair or replace the roof
  • Update outdated kitchens or bathrooms
  • Address code violations
  • Paint or make cosmetic improvements
  • Handle any deferred maintenance

In Texas, as-is sales are perfectly legal and quite common. However, you still have disclosure obligations under Texas Property Code. You must disclose known material defects, but you're not required to fix them before selling.

Is Selling As-Is Right for You?

Selling as-is to a cash buyer makes sense if you:

  • Need to sell quickly (within weeks rather than months)
  • Don't have funds or time for repairs
  • Want to avoid the hassle of showings and staging
  • Prefer certainty over potentially getting a higher price
  • Are dealing with a difficult property situation
  • Want to minimize stress during an already challenging time

However, if you have time, your home is in good condition, and you want to maximize your sale price, listing with a realtor might yield better results. There's no single right answer—it depends on your specific circumstances.
